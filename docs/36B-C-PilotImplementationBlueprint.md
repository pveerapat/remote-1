# 1. Purpose

ARI #36B defined the long-term architecture of an Agricultural Intelligence Cooperative Network.

The purpose of this document is to define how ARI launches and validates the first real-world pilot implementation.

The objective is not national scale.

The objective is proving that the model works.

---

# 2. Strategic Objective

Validate the progression:

Farm Value

↓

Community Value

↓

Economic Value

↓

Network Value

before scaling.

ARI should demonstrate that agricultural intelligence, community participation, and economic collaboration can create measurable value for participating farms.

---

# 3. Design Philosophy

ARI should not attempt to onboard thousands of farms immediately.

The pilot exists to validate assumptions, collect evidence, and generate practical operational knowledge.

Pilot execution shall prioritize:

* Learning speed
* Farmer trust
* Data quality
* Operational simplicity
* Economic validation

over technical sophistication.

Scaling decisions shall be evidence-driven.

---

# 4. Pilot Design Principles

## Why Pilot First

The pilot exists to validate:

* Farm value
* Community value
* Economic value
* Network value

before broader deployment.

## What Must Be Validated

* Farmer adoption
* Data collection capability
* Intelligence usefulness
* Community participation
* Economic benefits
* Trust formation

## What Should Not Be Optimized Initially

* National scale
* Full automation
* Advanced AI capabilities
* Large organizational structures

## Fail-Fast Principles

* Validate assumptions early
* Measure real outcomes
* Identify adoption barriers quickly
* Learn from failures

## Evidence-Driven Scaling

Scaling decisions must be based on demonstrated outcomes rather than projections.

---

# 5. Pilot Geography Selection Framework

Pilot geography should be selected using the following criteria:

## Agricultural Factors

* Durian concentration
* Farm density
* Water availability

## Community Factors

* Existing farmer relationships
* Community cohesion
* Presence of respected local leaders

## Infrastructure Factors

* Mobile connectivity
* Road access
* Logistics accessibility

## Economic Factors

* Market access
* Supplier accessibility
* Service provider availability

## Ideal Pilot Characteristics

An ideal pilot area should:

* Have a meaningful concentration of durian farms
* Possess existing community relationships
* Be logistically accessible
* Support regular field engagement

---

# 6. Pilot Farm Selection Model

Pilot farms should include a balanced representation of:

* Lead farms
* Average farms
* Small farms
* Progressive farms
* Traditional farms

## Selection Criteria

* Willingness to participate
* Commitment to data collection
* Accessibility
* Ability to complete a full production season

## Minimum Requirements

* Active farm operation
* Farmer participation
* Basic communication capability

## Data Readiness

Not required at advanced levels.

ARI should support farms with low digital maturity.

## Commitment Requirements

Farmers should agree to:

* Participate in pilot activities
* Share operational observations
* Contribute data during the pilot period

---

# 7. Community Champion Framework

## Purpose

Community Champions act as local trust anchors.

## Responsibilities

* Encourage participation
* Share knowledge
* Facilitate communication
* Support onboarding

## Selection Criteria

* Trusted by local farmers
* Good communication skills
* Positive reputation

## Incentives

To be determined based on pilot outcomes.

## Training

Champions should receive onboarding and operational guidance.

## Governance

Champions support participation but do not control farmer data ownership.

## Strategic Importance

Community leaders are essential for scaling trust-based adoption.
